Website's Initial sketches

The reason I chose computer science is because I wanted to create cool stuff. Making personal projects is a way I have to make cool stuff. I had the idea of making a portfolio website after attending a presentation at my university about making personal projects, in which a website was the two presenters' common project. I chose it as my first project in computer science after a lot of deabte, as it's a classic computer science project, and opens an area for me to showcase my future projects. As such, on April 3rd 2025, I started working on this project.

My first priority was to make a bunch of design decisions. As someone who had zero web development experience, I was immediately intimidated by the amount of different options I was presented with for everything. I settled on writing everything from scratch using HTML/CSS, as this way, I can focus on the very fundamentals I needed work on. I chose to host the final website on GitHub Pages, as it was free for me and on a platform I was already using, and had all the features I needed.

After that, I started making a project plan. My initial plan included a home page that mostly featured the latest highlights, an about section with a more detailed view of who I am, a Blog section where I would post updates that aren't related to computer science, a Project section that would eventually become an Experience section where I would post updates that are related to Computer Science and professional development as a whole, and a Contact Page, so that anyone can contact me. I also put in a lot of very ambitious ideas, such as an entire system to post new blog/project posts, a chibi character that runs around while you're on the website, and a background video that I would edit onto the home page that would look super cinematic. Most of those got scrapped from the initial release, but may be featured when I get the motivation/experience needed to work on my website 2.0

The choice to make it a pixel art theme came pretty early on. I am personally a big fan of pixel art, as I find a certain charm to it. It's a situation similar to how people love Pointillism art. It would also give my website a more personal style, as pixel art is not a theme I saw on many portfolio websites. It might also have been sunk-cost fallacy getting to me, as I had purchased a pixel art software two weeks before I began this project and never used it.

Ending of a level from the mod Strawberry Jam for the game Celeste, one of my favorite scenes in any video game

After that, I started learning. I spent 2 days watching HTML/CSS tutorials on youtube before I realized it was a bit of a waste of time past the first 3 hours and decided to take a more hands on approach. During that time, I set up my IDE (Visual Studio Code) for web development and learned that I didn't need to learn much JavaScript to accomplish 90% of the goals I set for myself for my website, which was a bit of a relief.

Progress went by pretty quick at first. Within the first week, I wrote HTML skeletons for the home page and about page, and had made some very nice looking styling. I also started searching for the fonts that I would use in the website, and eventually settled on three: Connection II, which resembles the minecraft font a lot, OS X Darwin, which ressembles more what you find on a more classic arcade, and Joystix, which also ressembled an arcade font, but gave different vibes. Although they ended up swapping a bunch, I decided to use Joystix for major titles, OS X Darwin for non-title text that were still kinda important, and Connection II for general text.

Soon however, progress started slowing down. There were two major reasons for that. First, I chose my timing extremely poorly as I had started this project right before final exam season, so I had to shift my focus onto my exams, and focus on moving out afterwards, as I was living in university residences. Second, I got stuck with a lot of artistic decisions such as choosing color schemes, picking how to place images and balancing all the artistic decisions with my capabilities as a web developper. Therefore, during the second half of April, I made very little progress on this website

Eventually however, all of those were over, but by then, a month had passed since I started this project. During that month, I got other smaller project ideas I wanted to work on, other concepts and tools I wanted to learn, which ultimately sapped my motivation of remaining on this project. This is around when I decided to scrap pretty much all my most ambitious ideas and shelve them for a later date, and why this project is titled "Personal Website 1.0". With a much smaller scope, I worked on finishing the last details before I would release my website officially. I drew the contact icons in my pixel art software to add them at the bottom in the contact area, I finished the entirety of the About section and most of the homepage, and I properly reformatted my file structures to make them easier to modify in the future.

And with that, and a long time styling, trying and rejecting designs, and struggling with thematic decisions, I released the website to the world, which is where you're reading this text right now!

The version I released on May 9th was definitely not fully polished. Some of the colors could've been chosen better, and a lot of the formatting break on mobile or other screen sizes. However, I'm releasing it now to officially stop making it my main focus, as I want to get started with other project ideas. I will still update it slowly in the future, though. Eventually, I also want to remake this website, which will probably be website version 2.0, using more framework and Javascript once I get more experienced with more concepts.

I hope you enjoyed reading through my journey of making this website!
